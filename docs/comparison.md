# Econumo compared

Econumo is not the only way to budget, and it is not the right tool for everyone.
These pages compare it against the alternatives people most often weigh it
against — on licence, price, popularity, release history and features — and say
plainly where the other tool is the better choice.

Every figure is sourced and dated. Where a claim comes from a competitor's own
documentation, it is linked.

---

## Comparisons

- [Econumo vs Actual Budget](/docs/comparison/econumo-vs-actualbudget/) — the
  closest open-source match: also MIT, also envelope budgeting, also
  self-hostable. Differs sharply on multi-currency, family sharing and bank sync.
- [Econumo vs YNAB](/docs/comparison/econumo-vs-ynab/) — the app that made
  envelope budgeting mainstream. Proprietary, cloud-only and $109/year, with
  excellent bank sync and no multi-currency within a budget.
- [Econumo vs Firefly III](/docs/comparison/econumo-vs-firefly-iii/) — the most
  established self-hosted finance manager. A double-entry ledger rather than an
  envelope budgeter, and strictly single-user.
- [Econumo vs Maybe Finance](/docs/comparison/econumo-vs-maybe-finance/) — Maybe
  shut down in 2025 and its repo is archived. Where displaced users should go, and
  when the community fork Sure is the better answer than Econumo.
- [Econumo vs ezBookkeeping](/docs/comparison/econumo-vs-ezbookkeeping/) — the
  closest *technical* twin: also Go, also MIT, also tiny. But it has no budgeting
  features and no shared household data.
- [Econumo vs GnuCash](/docs/comparison/econumo-vs-gnucash/) — a 28-year-old
  desktop double-entry accounting package. Far deeper accounting; no envelope
  budgeting, no sharing, no mobile.

## How Econumo positions itself

Across every comparison, the same three differences keep appearing:

1. **Multi-currency is native.** Each account has its own currency, cross-currency
   transfers record both amounts, and budgets convert to a single currency. Several
   popular alternatives do not support this at all. See
   [Multi-Currency](/docs/user-guide/multi-currency/).
2. **Family sharing is built in.** You connect by invitation and set an access
   level per account and per budget — no identity provider to run. See
   [Shared Access](/docs/user-guide/shared-access/).
3. **Hosting is a one-time payment.** Self-hosting is free forever; the hosted
   cloud is a single $20 payment per user rather than a subscription. See
   [Editions](/docs/edition/).

And the recurring trade-off to be aware of:

- **There is no automatic bank synchronisation.** Econumo is manual-entry first,
  with [CSV import/export](/docs/user-guide/csv-import-export/) and a
  [REST API](/docs/api/) for bulk data. If automatic bank import is essential to
  you, several alternatives do it better — see the [FAQ](/docs/faq/) for why.
